✦   THE MUHURTHAM   ✦

Jilakara Bellam

✦ జీలకర్ర బెల్లం · 23 April 2026  ·  10:16 AM (Auspicious Moment)

At the sacred muhurtham of 10:16 AM, the bride and groom each place their hand on the other's head bearing a mix of cumin seeds (jilakara) and jaggery (bellam) — symbolising that life with this person will always be a perfect blend of strength and sweetness. The most intimate and defining moment of the Telugu wedding, witnessed in reverent silence by all gathered.

photo here

Mudu Mullu

✦ మూడు ముళ్ళు · Following the Muhurtham

The three sacred knots — mudu mullu — tied by the groom to the bride's maangalyam, each representing body, mind and soul. Every knot a vow; every vow, a lifetime.

photo here

Kalyanam — The Grand Wedding Ceremony

✦ కళ్యాణం · 23 April 2026  ·  Koratla, Karimnagar District

After the jilakara bellam muhurtham and mudu mullu, Dr. Saicharan and Padmaja take the sacred saptapadi — seven vows around the holy fire — blessed by family priests versed in Telangana's ancient Vaidic tradition. Expect the fragrance of jasmine, the sound of nadaswaram and conch shells, and the joyful ululations of the women of Karimnagar ringing through the mandapam as two families become one.
